COMPREHENSIVE PROTEIN SOURCES GUIDE

PROTEIN QUALITY METRICS

Biological Value (BV):
- Measures protein absorption and utilization
- Higher BV = better quality protein
- Whey protein: BV 104 (highest)
- Egg: BV 100
- Chicken/Fish: BV 79
- Paneer: BV 84
- Soy: BV 74
- Legumes: BV 50-70

Complete vs Incomplete Proteins:
- Complete: Contains all 9 essential amino acids (animal sources, soy, quinoa)
- Incomplete: Missing one or more amino acids (most plant sources)
- Solution: Combine incomplete proteins (rice + dal = complete)

PROTEIN TIMING & DISTRIBUTION

Daily Distribution:
- Spread protein across 4-5 meals
- Each meal: 20-40g protein
- Maximizes muscle protein synthesis
- Better than consuming all protein in 1-2 meals

Optimal Timing:
Morning (Breakfast):
- 25-35g protein
- Example: 3 eggs + 1 cup milk

Pre-Workout:
- 15-25g protein
- Example: Banana + peanut butter

Post-Workout (Critical):
- 25-40g protein
- Fast-digesting preferred (whey, chicken)
- Example: Whey shake OR chicken breast

Before Bed:
- 20-30g slow-digesting protein
- Example: Paneer OR casein protein
- Prevents overnight muscle breakdown

PROTEIN ABSORPTION TIPS

Maximize Absorption:
- Don't exceed 40g protein per meal (excess not absorbed efficiently)
- Pair with vitamin C (improves iron absorption from plant proteins)
- Cook legumes properly (soaking + pressure cooking improves digestibility)
- Avoid excessive fiber with protein meals (can reduce absorption)

Digestibility:
- Whey protein: Fastest (absorbed in 30-60 mins)
- Chicken/Fish: Fast (2-3 hours)
- Paneer/Casein: Slow (4-6 hours)
- Legumes: Moderate (3-4 hours)

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

For Muscle Gain:
- Prioritize high BV proteins (eggs, chicken, whey, paneer)
- Aim for 2.0-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Include protein with every meal

For Fat Loss:
- Emphasize lean proteins (chicken breast, fish, egg whites, low-fat paneer)
- Protein has high thermic effect (burns calories during digestion)
- Increases satiety
- Aim for 1.8-2.4g per kg bodyweight

For Vegetarians:
- Combine incomplete proteins throughout the day
- Consider whey/casein supplementation
- Focus on paneer, dal, soy, Greek yogurt
- May need slightly higher total protein intake (2.0-2.5g/kg)
