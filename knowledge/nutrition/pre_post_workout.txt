PRE-WORKOUT & POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION GUIDE

PRE-WORKOUT NUTRITION

Timing:
- Eat 1-2 hours before training
- Allows digestion, prevents stomach discomfort
- Provides sustained energy during workout

Macronutrient Goals:
- Moderate carbohydrates (30-50g)
- Moderate protein (15-25g)
- Low fat (keeps digestion fast)
- Total: 200-400 kcal

Why Carbs Pre-Workout:
- Glycogen is primary fuel for intense training
- Prevents muscle breakdown during workout
- Improves performance and endurance
- Delays fatigue

Indian Pre-Workout Meal Options:

1-2 Hours Before:
- 2 rotis + paneer sabzi (light oil)
- Poha with peanuts
- Oats with banana and honey
- Rice with dal (small portion)
- Idli (2-3) with sambar

30-60 Minutes Before (Quick Options):
- Banana + peanut butter (1 tbsp)
- Apple + handful of almonds
- Dates (3-4) + milk
- Banana + whey shake
- Toast with jam

Hydration:
- Drink 300-500ml water 30 mins before
- Sip water during workout
- Avoid overhydration (causes stomach discomfort)

What to Avoid Pre-Workout:
- Heavy, fatty foods (slow digestion)
- Large meals (causes sluggishness)
- High-fiber foods (may cause GI distress)
- Spicy foods (heartburn risk)

POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION

The Anabolic Window:
- 30-120 minutes after training
- Muscles are most receptive to nutrients
- Glycogen replenishment is fastest
- Muscle protein synthesis is elevated

Macronutrient Goals:
- High protein (25-40g)
- Fast-digesting carbs (40-80g)
- Low-moderate fat
- Total: 300-600 kcal

Why Protein Post-Workout:
- Repairs muscle damage from training
- Stimulates muscle protein synthesis
- Prevents muscle breakdown
- Optimal dose: 0.3-0.5g per kg bodyweight

Why Carbs Post-Workout:
- Replenishes depleted glycogen stores
- Insulin spike helps shuttle nutrients to muscles
- Reduces cortisol (stress hormone)
- Improves recovery

Indian Post-Workout Meal Options:

Immediate (Within 30 mins):
- Whey protein shake + banana
- Chocolate milk (250ml) + 2 eggs
- Fruit smoothie with whey protein
- Banana + peanut butter + milk

Within 1-2 Hours (Full Meal):
- Chicken breast (150g) + rice (1 cup) + vegetables
- Paneer bhurji (150g) + 2 rotis
- Fish curry + rice
- Egg bhurji (4 eggs) + 2 rotis + fruit
- Dal (1 cup) + rice + paneer (100g)

Vegetarian Post-Workout:
- Whey shake + banana + oats
- Paneer tikka + rice
- Soy chunks curry + roti
- Greek yogurt + granola + honey + berries
- Tofu scramble + toast

Non-Vegetarian Post-Workout:
- Grilled chicken + sweet potato
- Tuna sandwich (whole wheat)
- Salmon + rice + broccoli
- Boiled eggs (4) + banana + toast

WORKOUT DURATION CONSIDERATIONS

Short Workout (<45 mins):
- Pre-workout: Light snack (banana)
- Post-workout: Regular meal within 2 hours

Moderate Workout (45-75 mins):
- Pre-workout: Moderate meal 1-2 hours before
- Post-workout: Protein + carbs within 1 hour

Long/Intense Workout (>75 mins):
- Pre-workout: Substantial meal 2 hours before
- Intra-workout: Consider BCAA or carb drink
- Post-workout: Large protein + carb meal immediately

TRAINING TIME VARIATIONS

Morning Training (Fasted):
- Some prefer fasted cardio for fat loss
- For strength training, at least eat banana + coffee
- Post-workout becomes breakfast (prioritize protein)

Evening Training:
- Lunch should be substantial (4-5 hours before)
- Light pre-workout snack 1 hour before
- Post-workout dinner within 1 hour

SUPPLEMENT CONSIDERATIONS

Effective Pre-Workout Supplements:
- Caffeine (coffee/green tea): Improves focus, energy
- Creatine (5g): Improves strength, power
- Beta-alanine: Delays muscle fatigue

Effective Post-Workout Supplements:
- Whey protein: Fast-digesting, convenient
- Creatine (5g): Muscle recovery and growth
- Glutamine: May reduce muscle soreness

Not Essential But Helpful:
- BCAAs: Useful if training fasted
- Carb powder (dextrose/maltodextrin): Fast glycogen replenishment

HYDRATION STRATEGY

Pre-Workout:
- 300-500ml water 30 mins before
- Well-hydrated urine should be pale yellow

During Workout:
- Sip 150-250ml every 15-20 mins
- For workouts >60 mins, consider electrolyte drink

Post-Workout:
- Drink 500-750ml water
- Rehydrate fully within 2 hours
- Monitor urine color

COMMON MISTAKES

Pre-Workout Mistakes:
- Training on completely empty stomach (low energy)
- Eating too close to workout (stomach discomfort)
- Too much fat/fiber (slow digestion)
- Not hydrating properly

Post-Workout Mistakes:
- Skipping post-workout meal (missed recovery window)
- Only protein, no carbs (incomplete recovery)
- Waiting too long to eat (>2 hours)
- Choosing slow-digesting proteins (casein, paneer)

GOAL-SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS

For Muscle Gain:
- Pre: Moderate carbs + protein
- Post: High protein (30-40g) + high carbs (60-80g)
- Don't skip post-workout meal
- Consider mass gainer if struggling to eat enough

For Fat Loss:
- Pre: Light snack (banana + coffee)
- Post: High protein (30-40g) + moderate carbs (30-50g)
- Still eat post-workout (preserves muscle)
- Avoid liquid calories unless necessary

For Endurance:
- Pre: Higher carbs (50-80g)
- During: Carb drink for sessions >90 mins
- Post: Carb-focused with moderate protein

PRACTICAL MEAL PREP TIPS

Batch Prep:
- Cook chicken/paneer in bulk on weekends
- Pre-portion rice/rotis
- Keep bananas, peanut butter readily available
- Prepare whey shakes in advance (dry powder in shaker)

On-the-Go Options:
- Protein bars (check sugar content)
- Roasted chana + banana
- Peanut butter sandwich
- Boiled eggs (pre-cooked)
- Whey shake (portable)

TIMING SUMMARY

Pre-Workout Timeline:
- 2 hours before: Full meal (carbs + protein)
- 1 hour before: Light meal/snack
- 30 mins before: Quick carbs (banana, dates)

Post-Workout Timeline:
- 0-30 mins: Liquid meal (shake) or quick snack
- 30-120 mins: Full solid meal
- Beyond 2 hours: Benefits diminish but still eat
