FAT LOSS NUTRITION GUIDELINES

CALORIC DEFICIT
- Target: 300-500 kcal deficit below maintenance
- Aggressive deficit (>500 kcal) risks muscle loss
- Track weight weekly: aim for 0.5-1kg loss per week
- If no weight loss after 2 weeks, reduce calories by 100-200 kcal
- Never go below 1200 kcal (women) or 1500 kcal (men)

PROTEIN REQUIREMENTS (CRITICAL FOR FAT LOSS)
- Target: 1.8-2.4g protein per kg bodyweight
- Higher protein preserves muscle during deficit
- For a 70kg individual: 126-168g protein per day
- Protein has high thermic effect (burns calories during digestion)
- Increases satiety and reduces hunger

MACRONUTRIENT DISTRIBUTION
- Protein: 30-40% of total calories (muscle preservation)
- Carbohydrates: 30-40% of total calories (energy for workouts)
- Fats: 20-30% of total calories (minimum for hormones)

SATIETY-FOCUSED FOODS
High Volume, Low Calorie:
- Vegetables (spinach, broccoli, cauliflower, cucumber)
- Salads with minimal dressing
- Clear soups (vegetable soup, dal soup)
- Fruits (watermelon, papaya, oranges)

High Protein, Low Fat:
- Chicken breast (skinless)
- Fish (white fish, tuna)
- Egg whites
- Low-fat paneer
- Greek yogurt (low-fat)
- Whey protein isolate

INDIAN LOW-CALORIE MEALS
Breakfast Options:
- Poha with vegetables (light oil): ~200 kcal
- Moong dal chilla: ~150 kcal
- Oats upma: ~180 kcal
- Egg white omelette with vegetables: ~120 kcal

Lunch/Dinner Options:
- Grilled chicken + salad + 1 roti: ~350 kcal
- Dal + sabzi + 1 roti: ~300 kcal
- Fish curry (light) + vegetables: ~280 kcal
- Paneer tikka + salad: ~250 kcal

Snacks:
- Roasted chana: ~120 kcal per 30g
- Cucumber/carrot sticks: ~20 kcal
- Apple: ~80 kcal
- Buttermilk (chaas): ~40 kcal

MEAL TIMING FOR FAT LOSS
- Eat protein with every meal (controls hunger)
- Front-load calories (bigger breakfast/lunch, lighter dinner)
- Stop eating 2-3 hours before bed
- Consider intermittent fasting (16:8) if it fits lifestyle

FOODS TO MINIMIZE
- Fried foods (samosa, pakora, bhujia)
- Refined carbs (white bread, maida products)
- Sugary drinks (soda, packaged juices)
- High-calorie Indian sweets (gulab jamun, jalebi)
- Excessive oil in cooking (use spray or measure)

METABOLIC RATE CONSIDERATIONS
- Don't crash diet (slows metabolism)
- Include 1-2 refeed days per week (eat at maintenance)
- Prioritize strength training to preserve muscle mass
- Muscle burns more calories at rest than fat
- Sleep 7-8 hours (poor sleep increases hunger hormones)

HYDRATION
- Drink 3-4 liters water daily
- Water before meals increases satiety
- Green tea or black coffee (no sugar) can boost metabolism slightly

HUNGER MANAGEMENT
- Eat high-fiber vegetables with meals
- Drink water when hungry (sometimes it's thirst)
- Chew food slowly (20+ minutes per meal)
- Use smaller plates (psychological portion control)
- Plan meals in advance to avoid impulsive eating

TRACKING TIPS
- Weigh yourself same time weekly (morning, after bathroom)
- Take progress photos every 2 weeks
- Measure waist circumference
- Don't obsess over daily weight fluctuations (water retention varies)

SUSTAINABILITY
- Allow 1 cheat meal per week (not cheat day)
- Focus on whole foods 80-90% of the time
- Build habits, not just follow a diet
- Fat loss is a marathon, not a sprint
