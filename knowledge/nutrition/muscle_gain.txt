MUSCLE GAIN NUTRITION GUIDELINES

PROTEIN REQUIREMENTS
- Target: 1.6-2.2g protein per kg bodyweight daily
- For a 70kg individual: 112-154g protein per day
- For muscle building, aim for the higher end (2.0-2.2g/kg)
- Distribute protein evenly across 4-5 meals (25-40g per meal)
- Post-workout protein: 20-40g within 2 hours of training

CALORIC SURPLUS
- Maintain a moderate surplus of 300-500 kcal above maintenance
- Aggressive surplus (>500 kcal) leads to excessive fat gain
- Track weight weekly: aim for 0.25-0.5kg gain per week
- If no weight gain after 2 weeks, increase calories by 200 kcal

MACRONUTRIENT DISTRIBUTION
- Protein: 25-30% of total calories
- Carbohydrates: 45-55% of total calories (fuel for training)
- Fats: 20-30% of total calories (hormone production)

INDIAN HIGH-PROTEIN FOODS
Vegetarian Sources:
- Paneer: 18g protein per 100g
- Tofu: 8g protein per 100g
- Moong dal (cooked): 7g protein per 100g
- Chana (chickpeas): 9g protein per 100g
- Greek yogurt: 10g protein per 100g
- Whey protein: 24g protein per 30g scoop
- Eggs: 6g protein per egg

Non-Vegetarian Sources:
- Chicken breast: 31g protein per 100g
- Fish (salmon, tuna): 20-25g protein per 100g
- Eggs: 6g protein per egg
- Mutton (lean): 25g protein per 100g

MEAL TIMING FOR MUSCLE GAIN
Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before):
- Moderate carbs + moderate protein
- Example: 2 rotis + paneer sabzi OR banana + peanut butter

Post-Workout (within 2 hours):
- High protein + fast-digesting carbs
- Example: Whey shake + banana OR chicken + rice

Before Bed:
- Slow-digesting protein (casein or paneer)
- Prevents overnight muscle breakdown

SAMPLE MEAL STRUCTURE
Breakfast: High protein + complex carbs
- Oats with whey protein, banana, nuts
- OR Egg bhurji with 2 rotis

Mid-Morning Snack:
- Greek yogurt with fruits
- OR Protein shake

Lunch: Balanced macros
- Chicken/paneer curry + rice + dal + salad
- OR Rajma rice with curd

Evening Snack (Pre-Workout):
- Banana + peanut butter
- OR Chana chaat

Dinner: Moderate carbs, high protein
- Grilled fish/chicken + vegetables + 1-2 rotis
- OR Dal + paneer + sabzi + roti

HYDRATION
- Minimum 3-4 liters water daily
- More during training days
- Dehydration impairs muscle recovery

CONSISTENCY TIPS
- Meal prep on weekends for busy weekdays
- Keep protein sources readily available
- Track intake for first 2-4 weeks to learn portions
- Don't skip meals, especially post-workout
